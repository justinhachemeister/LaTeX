\begin{problem}{Fulton Chapter 3 Property 6}{}
	If $F=\prod F_i^{r_i}$, and $G=\prod G_j^{s_j}$, then $I(P, F \cap G)=\sum\limits_{i, j} r_i s_j I\left(P, F_i \cap G_j\right)$
\end{problem}

\solve{
	It is enough to show that $$I(P,F\cap GH)=I(P,F\cap G)+I(P,F\cap H)$$if one of $I(P,F\cap G)$ or $I(P,F\cap H)$ is infinite then $F$ has a common component with $G$ or $H$. So, $F$ has a common component with $GH$ so, $I(P,F\cap GH)=\infty$
\setlength{\parindent}{1cm}

Assume that both of $I(P,F\cap G)$ or $I(P,F\cap H)$ is finite.$$F,GH\in (F,G)\implies (F,GH)\subseteq (F,G)\qquad O=O_P(A^2)$$
so, the natural map $\phi$ from $O/(F,GH)$ to $O/(F,G)$ is onto $ \psi:O/(F,H)\to O/(F,GH)$ s.t. $\psi(\overline{z})=\overline{Gz}$. 
So clearly $\psi$ is a $k-$ linear map.

Now $$\forall\ \overline{z}\in O/(F,H)\phi(\psi(\overline{z}))=\phi(\overline{Gz})=0$$ in $O/(F,G)$ [As $Gz\in (G)\subseteq (F,G)$]. So, $\im(\psi)\subseteq ker(\phi)$

If $$\overline{z}\in O/(F,GH)\in ker (\phi)\implies z\in (F,G)\implies z=aF+bG$$
so, $\psi(\overline{b})=\overline{bG}=\overline{z}$ so, im$(\psi)= ker(\phi)$. Let $\psi(\overline{z})=0$. So, $$Gz\in(F,GH)\implies Gz=a_1F+a_2GH$$
Now $\exists\ D\in k[x,y]$ s.t. $$Dz=c;Da_1=b_1;Da_2=b_2$$ where $c,b_1,b_2\in k[x,y]$ and $D(P)\neq 0$. So, 
\begin{multline*}
Gc=b_1F+b_2GH\implies G(b_2H-c)=b_1F\implies F|(b_2H-c)\\
\implies b_2H-c=Fr\quad [\text{As } F,G\text{ has no common factor}]
\end{multline*}
So, $z=a_2H-(r/D)F$ as $D(P)\neq 0\implies D$ is an unit in $O$. So, $z\in (F,H)$. So, $\psi $ is one-one. $$0\longrightarrow O/(F,H)\xrightarrow{\psi}O/(F,GH)\xrightarrow{\phi}O/(F,G)\longrightarrow0$$
this sequence is exact. So, $$dim_k(O/(F,GH)=dim_k(O/(F,G))+dim_k(O/(F,H))$$So,$$(P,F\cap GH)=I(P,F\cap G)+I(P,F\cap H)$$
